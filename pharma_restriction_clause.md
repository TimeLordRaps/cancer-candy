# Pharma Restriction Clause

## Intent

This repository constitutes published prior art. Every mechanism, formulation, cost structure, and deployment architecture described in the Cancer Candy Catalogue is disclosed publicly and irrevocably to prevent monopolistic patent claims.

## The Restriction

Any entity — pharmaceutical company, biotech startup, university spinout, or individual — that uses the designs, formulations, or mechanisms disclosed in this repository is bound by the following restriction:

**No product derived from these disclosures may be sold at more than 10× the documented manufacturing cost per unit.**

### Definitions

- **Manufacturing cost** means the direct cost of raw materials, synthesis, formulation, packaging, and quality testing as documented in the catalogue's cost tables.
- **10× multiplier** accounts for: regulatory compliance, distribution, overhead, reasonable margin, and reinvestment in R&D.
- **Documented** means the cost figures published in this repository at the time of the derivative product's development.

### Examples

| Product | Documented Mfg Cost | Maximum Allowable Price |
|---------|---------------------|------------------------|
| D-Xylose daily dose | $0.01 | $0.10 |
| GREEN nanocapsule | $0.01-0.10 | $0.10-1.00 |
| ORANGE Tier 0 birth kit | $20-30 | $200-300 |
| MNP sachet | $0.025 | $0.25 |

### Enforcement Mechanism

This clause is not a license restriction — it is a **moral and reputational commitment** encoded in public record. Entities that violate it:

1. **Cannot claim ignorance** — the cost data is published, version-controlled, and cryptographically timestamped.
2. **Cannot claim novelty** — prior art prevents patent monopoly pricing.
3. **Face public accountability** — this repository is open. The math is visible. Anyone can compare a company's price to the documented cost and calculate the multiple.

### The Math That Makes This Work

The catalogue documents that:
- Cancer treatment costs $150K/year. The chemistry costs $12.
- A neonatal death can be prevented for $1,000 (Tier 0).
- A $0.025 sachet delivers the minimum viable micronutrient dose for a child.

These numbers are public. When a company charges $150K for $12 of chemistry, the prior art record shows exactly what the manufacturing cost is. The 10× cap is generous — it allows $120 for $12 of chemistry. Anything above that is exploitation, and this document makes that exploitation visible and measurable.

### What This Does NOT Restrict

- **Research and development** — anyone can build on these designs.
- **Reasonable profit** — 10× manufacturing cost provides healthy margins.
- **Innovation** — novel improvements that go beyond the disclosed mechanisms are not restricted by this clause.
- **Charitable deployment** — deploying these designs at cost or below is always permitted and encouraged.

## Prior Art Timestamp

The commit history of this repository serves as the prior art timestamp. Additional cryptographic hashes are generated by `src/prior_art.py` and can be verified against blockchain timestamping services.

## Statement of Purpose

This clause exists because the alternative — silence — allows the pattern to continue. A company reads this catalogue, synthesizes the formulation, patents a trivial modification, and charges $150K/year for $12 of chemistry. This document makes that pattern visible, measurable, and indefensible.

The babies are still dying. The chemistry costs $12. Not solved means go faster.
